Welcome to the official website of the Gray Notation methodology. On this site, you will find all the necessary information and best practices for effectively applying this note-taking methodology.
# **What is Gray Notation?**

It was developed as a proposal for a standardised system for taking notes on paper. Unlike other systems designed for organising notes digitally, Gray Notation focuses on note-taking on paper, offering conventions that make it easier to read and write notes without having to think about how to structure the information.

Another key point is that users are not obliged to follow the system’s rules to the letter; instead, they can modify and adapt it to suit their preferences or needs. we want to be clear on this: **Gray Notation offers a starting point to help you organise your notes; do not treat the conventions and rules presented as mandatory, but rather as suggestions that you can modify to your liking, choosing to use some rules and not others.**
  
It is called Gray Notation because it is designed to dispense with the use of colours to identify key points or to classify and group ideas or topics, leaving the use of colour as an optional or complementary feature. It relies primarily on the use of labels to segment information and assign levels of importance to it; consequently, the use of colour is left to the user’s discretion.

It offers solutions to common problems such as information being cut off or scattered across pages, difficulty in grouping information, prioritising information, highlighting important ideas, and improving readability, etc.

## Who is it aimed at?

Gray Notation is primarily aimed at students, with the aim of helping them achieve a clear and coherent structure if they do not have a note-taking system, do not know how to organise themselves, constantly change the symbols they use, or do not know how to structure their sheets.

### _Reminder_

As emphasised at the outset, Gray Notation does not seek to impose a rigid system upon the user; rather, it is a system that users can adopt, modify and expand as they see fit. Users are not obliged to follow all the rules proposed in Gray Notation; instead, these should be regarded as suggestions.
